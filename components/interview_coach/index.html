Interview Success Tips for Veterans

Expert advice to help you stand out in civilian job interviews

Translate Military Jargon

Avoid military acronyms and terminology that civilian employers may not understand. Practice explaining your experience in civilian-friendly language before your interview.

Use the STAR Method

Structure your answers using the Situation, Task, Action, Result format to provide clear, concise examples of your accomplishments and skills.

Highlight Transferable Skills

Emphasize skills like leadership, teamwork, problem-solving, and adaptability that are highly valued in civilian workplaces.

Quantify Your Achievements

Include specific numbers, percentages, and metrics when describing your accomplishments to demonstrate tangible results.

Research the Company

Demonstrate your interest by researching the company's mission, values, products, and recent news before your interview.
