How ATS Scanning Works

Understanding how employers use Applicant Tracking Systems

What is an ATS?

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are software used by 98% of Fortune 500 companies to filter resumes before they reach human recruiters. These systems scan for keywords, formatting, and qualifications to determine which candidates move forward.

How ATS Filters Work

ATS systems parse your resume into searchable fields, looking for specific keywords and phrases that match the job description. Resumes that don't meet a certain threshold of relevance are automatically rejected.

Why ATS Optimization Matters

Up to 75% of qualified candidates are rejected by ATS systems before a human ever sees their resume. Military veterans often use terminology that doesn't align with civilian job descriptions, making ATS optimization especially important.

Improving Your Success Rate

Our ATS Scanner analyzes your resume against specific job descriptions, providing actionable feedback to increase your chances of getting past automated filters and into the hands of hiring managers.

ATS Optimization Tips

Expert advice to help your resume pass through ATS filters

01

Use Standard Section Headings

Use clear, standard section headings like "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills" that ATS systems are programmed to recognize.

02

Incorporate Relevant Keywords

Include industry-specific keywords and phrases from the job description, but avoid "keyword stuffing" which can trigger spam filters.

03

Avoid Complex Formatting

Skip tables, headers/footers, text boxes, and graphics. Use simple bullet points and standard fonts that ATS systems can easily parse.

04

Use Standard File Formats

Submit your resume as a .docx or .pdf file, as these formats are most compatible with ATS systems. Avoid image files like .jpg or .png.

05

Spell Out Acronyms

Include both the spelled-out version and the acronym for military terms and industry-specific abbreviations (e.g., "Supply Chain Management (SCM)").
